Vestibule of Hell

Hell's Gateway

"Lasciate ogne speranza, voi ch'intrate," says the inscription at the Gates of Hell, meaning "abandon all hope, ye who enter here." There is no hope for the souls in Hell, but there is still hope for you, the travelerâ€”precisely why visiting here is so important. In this vestibule, you can find the souls of the uncommitted, the ones who took no sides in life, being punished. You can also find the river Acheron, which you can cross using a ferry piloted by the notoriously obstinate Charon.

Dealing with Charon

Passage rights through the Acheron are given exclusively to Charon, the ferryman you'll find at the docks of the vestibule. Unfortunately, some travelers have reported Charon being difficult to work with, as he protests the living entering Hell. Luckily, we have found a fix for if he acts like this: if you tell him your visited is "willed on divine grounds," he should allow you to enter his boat.

Tip: Bugs in the Vestibule

Be careful of all of the critters around the vestibule! Wasps and maggots chase and feed on the uncommitted; you can watch, but don't get close or stare for too long, or you could become their next target. Make sure to pack bug spray!
