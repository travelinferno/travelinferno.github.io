First Circle: Limbo

Nothing but the Greats

Compared to the rest of Hell, Limbo may seem pretty boring to a traveler. But it can be one of the most engaging places to visit if you are willing to interact with the locals. Limbo houses all of the righteous figures who did not embrace Christ, including all of the virtuous born before Christ. Homer, Virgil, Hector, Aeneas, Caesar, Latinus, Aristotle, Socrates, Plato, Diogenes, Euclid, and more of the ancient world's greatest men can be found here. They have been reported to be friendly and insightful in conversation, so feel free to walk up to these figures to instigate a discussion if you wish. Be careful, though! Time stands still and your actions in life are immortalized down here; don't try to talk to Camilla about Aeneas, for example.

Tip: Christ's Descent to Limbo

In Limbo, you will encounter a lot of souls from before the time of Christ, but there is one group you shouldn't bother searching forâ€”most of the Jewish figures in the Old Testament. Legend has it that after Jesus Christ was crucified, he descended into Limbo and took the Jewish heroes and prophets, such as Adam, Abraham, Moses, and David, into Heaven with him before his resurrection.

Heaven or Hell?

You will probably notice that Limbo is the only part of Hell with no punishment. Some have said it's more akin to a sort of Heaven than Hell. This isn't surprising, though; remember that Hell is God's divine justice for the people who do not belong in Heaven. While the people in Limbo have not sinned, they have not embraced Christ, and are thus not welcome in God's kingdom. So you wouldn't be wrong to say Limbo is like a less glorious Heaven!
