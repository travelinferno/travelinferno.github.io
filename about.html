Hello!

My name is Adam Godel and I am a first-year student at Boston University studying mathematics and computer science. I created this website for my CC102 class as a project on Dante's Inferno. I was inspired by the 1859 painting Dante and Virgil by Jean-Baptiste-Camille Corot, which is currently on display at the Boston Museum of Fine Arts [75.2]. I decided to transform the painting into a justification for traveling to Hell as a way to conquer the beasts of sin Dante encounters at the beginning of the text. This website, Travel Inferno, is the result, serving as a fictional travel website for prospective tourists planning to visit Hell. As images within the website, I also used various artistic works by Sandro Botticelli, Stradanus, Gustave Dor√©, Auguste Rodin, Ary Scheffer, Joseph Anton Koch, and William Blake.

This is my first major web development project and was not created to function as any sort of professional or rigorously made website, but rather a way for me to try working with a larger project using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. While I am a proficient programmer, my main interest is currently quantum computing and this project was a fun way to step out of my comfort zone.
